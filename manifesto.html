MANIFESTO

When the Supreme Court of the United States reaches a decision, the justices summarize the result in open court to the lucky few who are in the courtroom. The Court records these events, but does not post these opinion announcements on its website. Instead, months later, it provides the National Archives and Records Administration (NARA) with copies of all public proceedings. The Court does not allow cameras in the courtroom. No videos or photographs exist of the impactful Supreme Court decisions that shape our society. On the Docket broadens access to these critically important moments. Our mission is to create more engaged and informed citizens. Starting with the most important recent decisions, we have transformed the justices' recordings into compelling video content. With the help of A.I. production, we have created visual experiences that make these decisions accessible and engaging for the public.

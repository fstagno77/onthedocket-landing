PRIVACY AND COOKIE POLICY

Last updated: February 5, 2026

This privacy and cookie policy explains how On the Docket ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and protects your personal information when you visit onthedocket.org (the "Site") and interact with our services. We are committed to transparency about our data practices and to respecting your privacy rights under applicable laws, including the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and United States state privacy laws such as the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA).

The data controller for the purposes of this policy is On the Docket, LLC. For any questions or requests regarding your personal data, you can reach us at contact@onthedocket.org.

INFORMATION WE COLLECT

We collect personal information in the following ways.

Information you provide directly. When you subscribe to our newsletter through the signup form on our Site, we collect your email address. Newsletter subscriptions are managed through Mailchimp and use a double opt-in process, meaning you will receive a confirmation email and must verify your address before being added to our mailing list.

Information collected automatically. When you visit our Site, certain information is collected automatically through cookies and similar tracking technologies. This includes your IP address, browser type and version, operating system, referring URL, pages visited, time spent on pages, scroll depth, and interactions with page elements such as buttons and links. This information is collected through Google Analytics 4 (GA4), managed via Google Tag Manager (GTM).

HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION

We use the information we collect for the following purposes: to send you newsletters and updates about new content, case analyses, and project developments (only if you have subscribed); to understand how visitors interact with our Site so we can improve its content, structure, and user experience; to measure the effectiveness of our content distribution across platforms; and to comply with legal obligations and respond to lawful requests.

We do not sell your personal information to third parties. We do not use your information for automated decision-making or profiling.

COOKIES AND TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. We use cookies and similar technologies to analyze Site traffic and understand visitor behavior.

Strictly necessary cookies. These cookies are essential for the Site to function properly and for the cookie consent system to remember your preferences. They cannot be disabled.

Analytics cookies. We use Google Analytics 4 to collect anonymized usage data about how visitors interact with our Site. GA4 cookies include _ga (used to distinguish unique users, expires after 2 years) and _ga_[container-id] (used to persist session state, expires after 2 years). Google Analytics may transfer data to servers in the United States. Google's privacy policy is available at policies.google.com/privacy.

Cookie consent management. We use iubenda Privacy Controls and Cookie Solution to manage your cookie preferences. When you first visit the Site, a banner will ask for your consent before any non-essential cookies are placed on your device. You can change your preferences at any time through the cookie settings link available on every page of the Site.

How to manage cookies. In addition to the iubenda consent tool, you can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies, delete existing cookies, or be notified before a cookie is set. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of certain parts of the Site.

DATA RETENTION

Newsletter subscriber data is retained for as long as you remain subscribed. You can unsubscribe at any time using the link included in every email we send, and your data will be removed from our active mailing list within 30 days of your request.

Analytics data collected through Google Analytics is retained for 14 months from the date of collection, after which it is automatically deleted.

YOUR RIGHTS UNDER THE GDPR

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights regarding your personal data: the right to access your data and receive a copy of it; the right to rectify inaccurate or incomplete data; the right to erasure of your data under certain circumstances; the right to restrict processing of your data; the right to data portability, allowing you to receive your data in a structured, commonly used format; the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests; and the right to withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at contact@onthedocket.org. We will respond to your request within 30 days. If you are unsatisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.
